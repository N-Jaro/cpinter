HOW TO APPLY

COURSE OVERVIEW

What Will You Study

Studying for a research degree is a highly rewarding and challenging process. You'll work to become a leading expert in your topic area with regular contact and close individual supervision with your supervisor. You'll have the opportunity to engage with like-minded individuals, pushing forward at the very edge of the discipline. Active seminar programmes and state of the art facilities mean there's plenty of ideas and technologies to stimulate your development as a research scientist. Research skills courses help support you in learning to improve your scientific methods, written and verbal communication skills, to help you realise the maximum potential from your work. Annual progress review panels will help to keep your research on track.

At Chulalongkorn University, we're immensely proud of our strong postgraduate research community and of the internationally significant reputation they've helped us achieve. You'll be encouraged to participate fully, targeting and presenting your work at major international conferences and workshops. There are numerous opportunities to collaborate nationally and internationally through our many research partnerships and links with industrial collaborators.

Key Information

Degree: Doctor of Philosophy, Ph.D. (Computer Engineering)

Program’s Code: 521100

Course Length: 3 Academic years

Director of Studies: Dr. Natawat Nupairoj

Entry requirements: Second class honours degree, or its equivalent, in Computer Engineering or a closely related discipline. Relevant industry experience may be considered

Assessment: Coursework and dissertation

Funding: All applicants should consult our information on scholarship and funding.

APPLICATION REQUIREMENTS

Admission Plans

This course has 2 plans

  1. Plan A(1) opens for Bachelor graduates in Computer Engineering degree-holder with second class honor or having at least 3.25 GPAX.
  2. Plan A(2) opens for Master of Engineering or Master of Science degree-holder in Computer, Physics, or Mathematics.

By the way, all applicants in both plans must achieve the qualifications below.

Ph.D. Qualifications

  1. University requirement on English language proficiency

    – minimum score: CUTEP 67 / TOEFL 525 (Paper-base) or 71 (Internet-based) / IELTS 5.5

    – minimum score: CUTEP 45 / TOEFL 450 (Paper-base) or 45 (Internet-based) / IELTS 4.0 (applicants will be required to register -costing no extra fee- in university’s English language courses if they are admitted into our graduate program at Chulalongkorn University)

    The applicant may be exempted from English placement test if he/she has the following qualification:

    1. Graduated from countries using English as official language.
    2. Graduated from Study program using English as medium of instruction at university which Civil Service Commission of Thailand has certified.

    Remark: scholarship requirement on English language proficiency and application deadline may be different from the information above. Please refer to the scholarship announcement for scholarship’s specific information at scholarship and funding.

  2. All required information must be provided before the application deadline:

    - Febuary (for admission into Master's program in the first semester : study period from August onwards)

    - September (for admission into Master's program in the second semester : study period from January onwards)

  3. Applicant must fill the Application form and follow the application process.

COURSE STRUCTURE

What You Can Focus On

You will choose to study a wide range of modules as part of your course, some examples of which are listed below.

  • Machine Learning
  • Machine Vision
  • Mobile Computing
  • Advanced Topics in Artificial Intelligence
  • Special Topics in Distributed Systems
  • Distributed Systems
  • Cryptography
  • Robotics

Information contained on the website with respect to modules is correct at the time of publication, but changes may be necessary, for example as a result of student feedback, Professional Statutory and Regulatory Bodies' (PSRB) requirements, staff changes, and new research.

Curriculum Organization

Plan A(1) has 72 total credits for graduation, consisting of

  1. Seminar Courses (Non-credit) (4 Courses)
  2. Required Courses 12 credits
  3. Electives 12 credits
  4. Dissertation 48 credits

Plan A(2) has 60 total credits for graduation, consisting of

  1. Seminar Courses (Non-credit) (4 Courses)
  2. Electives 12 credits
  3. Dissertation 48 credits
